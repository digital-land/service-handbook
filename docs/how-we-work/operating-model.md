This operating model describes how we work together as a team of teams to make planning and housing data easy to find, use and trust. It's a high-level description of 'our process' to show how we operate and manage the platform and its data. It gives a quick overview of the model, the key activities at each step, and the roles and responsibilities of each team.

The model follows the data lifecycle, from identifying a planning data need through to delivering trusted data on the platform. It ensures we build the right data, in the right way, with the right people – then scale and sustain it.
It's not a linear model: it is a continuous improvement loop, so that the platform and its data remain valuable, trustworthy, and aligned to policy and user needs.

## How to use

This page gives new recruits and our stakeholders an idea of how works flows from end to end. ## The steps explained

### 1. Collect, research and assess needs

We make sure we’re solving the right problem, grounded in real planning and user needs.

- Discover how each consideration plays an important role in planning decision-making and understand the needs behind the data.
- Gather information and evidence that will help us plan and prioritise which considerations to work on next.

### 2. Prioritise what to work on

We choose the highest‑value work and secure alignment with leadership and stakeholders.

- Form a pitch for which considerations will best meet the needs of the planning system and help the programme better meet its mission.
- Get the support of key stakeholders on our decisions for what to work on next.

### 3. Co-design, test and iterate

We design the minimum viable data model and specification, prove it works, then iterate fast with users.

- Co-design the smallest data model that will satisfy the highest number of needs with our users. Test the data model, proving its feasibility and value, and iterate the model where useful.
- Use an agile, user-centred approach to identify and mitigate any associated risks.
- Produce a technical specification and supporting materials needed by data providers and data consumers, and to support data management and benefits evaluation.

### 4. Piloting

We trial in the real world, improve what’s needed, and prepare to scale sustainably.

- Test the data model with a wider subset of users under real conditions. Understand the needs and experiences of data providers, building or iterating services which make data provision more sustainable.
- Identify 1 or 2 common needs and prioritise changes to the data model which can provide more value. Identify growth levers which can increase the speed and volume of data provision or consumption.
- Progress the consideration from pilot to stable and supported.
- Understand the scaling demands placed on the platform, its performance, and the support & operations team.

### 5. On the platform

The data becomes a supported 'product' on the platform – scaled up, maintained, monitored, and actively adopted.

- A planning consideration and its data is fully supported and available. We focus on increasing the coverage of data up to national scale, providing high value to data consumers.
- The data is actively maintained in accordance with the Government Data Quality Framework. Collaboration with data providers improves the quality of data, which increases its trustworthiness. Quality and freshness is actively monitored and improved.
- We engage with a wider set of potential data consumers to increase data usage, identifying unmet needs and opportunities to improve the benefits realised by the data.

### 6. Monitor success, gather feedback for iteration

We measure outcomes (adoption, quality, and freshness), learn from stakeholders and changes in policy, and feed improvements back into discovery and design.

- Monitor our impact and identify opportunities for making improvements and increasing value. This stage overlaps with the first, making a loop.
- Report on the adoption, freshness and quality of data, feeding into the benefits and economic models plus other governance processes.
- Liaise with policy regularly to learn about any consequential policy changes, e.g. new use classes or changes to Local Plans policy.
